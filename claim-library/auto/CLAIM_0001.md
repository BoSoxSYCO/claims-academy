# Claim 0001 – Rear-End Collision

Version: 1.0

---

## Learning Objective

Learn how to review a First Notice of Loss (FNOL), identify missing information, and develop an investigation plan before making any coverage or liability decisions.

## Assignment

You are the claims trainee assigned to this file.

Your supervisor has asked you to review the information currently available and determine what additional information is needed before making any decisions.

Do not assume facts that have not been provided.

---

## First Notice of Loss (FNOL)

At approximately 8:10 AM, John Carter reported that he rear-ended another vehicle while driving to work.

The accident occurred at the intersection of Oak Street and Pine Avenue.

The insured reports:

- He was traveling approximately 35 mph.
- The vehicle in front of him stopped suddenly.
- He was unable to stop before impact.
- Police responded to the scene.
- No ambulance was requested.
- Both vehicles were driven from the scene.

The claimant later reported neck pain but did not seek medical treatment at the scene.

## Instructor Notes

Primary Teaching Point:

New adjusters often decide liability too early.

The correct approach is to identify what is known, identify what is missing, and continue the investigation before reaching conclusions.
