# Pool Drainage Improvement Plan
## Report 03 — Final Fix Plan: Zero Flow Into the Pool Area

**Prepared by:** Residential Drainage Engineer (stormwater management, Florida)
**Date:** 2026-07-18
**Goal:** Eliminate all stormwater, sand, and sediment flow into the pool area.
**Basis:** 57 site photos + 2 heavy-rain storm videos. No survey elevations (see hose test).

---

### The core problem, restated

The full photo set shows the backyard is a **shallow bowl with the pool/deck at the lowest point.** Water reaches the pool from three directions at once:

1. **Offsite hill** behind the white vinyl fence (the biggest single source).
2. **The yard's own bowl** — the lawn is higher on essentially every side and pitches inward toward the deck.
3. **Roof downspouts** discharging near the house corner / pool equipment pad, right beside the deck.

There is **no working drain** at the deck edge — both sides of the pool are just **decorative rock and an edge** that the owner tried to use for drainage, and it doesn't work. Sand rides right over it and fans out on the deck by the spa.

**To get *zero* flow into the pool, two things must both be true:**
- **(A)** Nothing that lands uphill can reach the deck — it gets intercepted first.
- **(B)** The ground immediately around the deck slopes *away* from the deck, not toward it.

Interception alone won't do it while the bowl still tilts inward. Regrading alone won't do it while the hill keeps dumping volume. You need both.

---

### The plan — a 6-part system

```
        HILL behind vinyl fence
               │  (offsite water)
               ▼
   ①  FENCE-LINE INTERCEPTOR  ── cut the hill water at the top
               │
   ③  REGRADE  ── yard now falls AWAY from the deck, out to the sides
               │
   ②  PERIMETER "MOAT" DRAIN around the uphill sides of the deck
       (edge/channel drain at deck + French drain in the lawn)
               │        ▲
   ④  ROOF DOWNSPOUTS ─┘   (piped in, not dumped by the deck)
               │
   ⑤  CONVEY to the STREET (left) ── sediment sump → pop-up outlet
               │
   ⑥  STABILIZE soil + CLOSE knee-wall gaps  (stops the sand at the source)
```

#### ① Fence-line interceptor — *cut the incoming hill water*
A French drain (fabric-wrapped perforated pipe in washed gravel) and/or a shallow swale along the **base of the white vinyl fence**, on the uphill sides. This removes the offsite volume before it ever crosses the lawn. Highest-impact single element.

#### ② Perimeter "moat" drain around the deck — *the last line of defense*
A continuous collection line wrapping the **uphill sides of the pool deck** — the back and fence sides:
- **At the deck edge:** a slot/channel (trench) drain flush with the pavers, so any water arriving at the deck drops in instead of crossing onto it.
- **Just upslope in the lawn:** a shallow French drain feeding the same line.
This is **new construction** where today there is only decorative rock. With ① reducing the volume, this moat catches the rest. Nothing gets past it onto the deck.

#### ③ Regrade the yard — *break the bowl (non-negotiable)*
Re-shape the lawn so the ground **falls away from the deck** on all sides, toward the interceptor/moat lines and then toward the street. This is what fixes the root cause: after regrading, the yard no longer drains to the pool — it drains to the collection lines. Create a shallow **swale** along the side yard to carry water to the street.

#### ④ Capture the roof downspouts
Tie the downspout(s) near the equipment pad into **solid pipe** so roof water is carried away with the rest — not released a few feet from the deck.

#### ⑤ Convey everything to the street — *with a sediment trap*
All three sources (①, ②, ④) combine into a **solid pipe** run to the **street on the left** at proper slope (≥1%, ¼-in/ft ideal), through a **catch basin with a deep sediment sump** so sand settles where you can clean it out, ending in a **pop-up emitter** at the curb/right-of-way. Sediment never reaches the pool because it drops out in the sump.

#### ⑥ Stabilize the soil + close the gaps — *stop making sand*
Re-sod (or dense-plant) the bare sandy lawn with **erosion-control matting** on the slopes so the soil stops mobilizing, and **close the decorative knee-wall gaps** (especially by the spa) so nothing can shortcut onto the deck. This protects the whole system from silting up.

---

### Why this achieves *zero* flow to the pool

| Water source | What stops it |
|---|---|
| Hill behind fence | ① intercepts it at the top; ⑤ carries it to the street |
| Yard bowl / sheet flow | ③ regrades it away from the deck; ② catches whatever reaches the edge |
| Roof runoff | ④ pipes it away before it hits the yard |
| Sand / sediment | ⑥ stops it at the source; ⑤ sump traps whatever moves |

The deck ends up **downhill of nothing** and **ringed by a drain** — the two conditions required for no intrusion.

---

### Build sequence (biggest payoff first)

| Phase | Work | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Close knee-wall gaps; rebuild the deck-edge drain (②, deck portion); pipe the roof downspouts (④) | Immediate, visible reduction in deck→pool flow |
| **2** | Re-sod + erosion matting (⑥) | Stops most of the *sand* |
| **3** | Fence-line interceptor (①) | Removes the *hill water* at the source |
| **4** | Regrade the bowl + side-yard swale (③) and the lawn portion of the moat (②) | Breaks the root-cause geometry |
| **5** | Conveyance line + sediment sump + street outlet (⑤) | Ties it all together and carries it off-site |

Phases 1–2 alone will make a big dent (they attack the sediment directly). Phases 3–5 are what deliver *zero* flow in the heaviest storms.

---

### Two checks before digging

1. **$0 hose grade test:** run a hose at the side of the house and confirm water naturally heads toward the street. If yes, ⑤ discharges to the street. **If not**, ⑤ instead discharges to a **dry well** (a buried gravel/chamber infiltration pit) — your sandy soil drains well, so this is a strong backup. Everything else in the plan is identical.
2. **Before any excavation:** call **811 (Sunshine 811)** for utility locates, and confirm your county/city rules for discharging to the right-of-way on a corner lot.

---

### Bottom line

The fix is a **ring + regrade + route** system: intercept the hill water at the top, re-tilt the yard so it drains away from the deck, ring the uphill deck edge with a proper drain, and route everything (plus the roof downspouts) around the pool to the street with a sediment trap in line. Do that, and there is no path left for water or sand to reach the pool.
