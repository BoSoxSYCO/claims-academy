### Corrected diagnosis (from owner walk-through + hose test)

The earlier reports assumed an **offsite hill behind the vinyl fence** pushing water onto the property. **That is wrong.** The corrected picture:

- **No offsite water.** Nothing comes from behind/under the white vinyl fence. The vinyl fence is the **back property line**, and water there actually heads **out toward the street** (which is on the **left**).
- **Source #1 — the back strip.** Rain begins rolling about **4–5 ft out from the pool's retaining wall** (the back/fence side) and runs **over the wall into the pool.** Only that ~4–5 ft strip drains to the pool; beyond it, water goes to the fence/street on its own.
- **Source #2 — the downspouts.** Gutter downspouts at the **back corners of the house (~2–3 ft off the wall)** dump roof water right by the deck. The **neighbor-side downspout is by the pool pump** — the same spot the sediment sump will go.
- **No existing drain.** Both sides of the pool are decorative **rock + edge** the owner tried to use; it doesn't work.
- **Outlet = street (left), confirmed** by hose test (water runs toward the street on the tested side). Gravity works; **no dry well, no pump.**

### The revised fix — 4 parts + sod (much smaller than before)

| # | Element | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **①** | **Catch drain at the retaining wall** (channel/trench drain along the deck's back edge, buried, grate flush, with a sediment **sump**) | Grabs the 4–5 ft strip's runoff before it goes over the wall |
| **②** | **Regrade the 4–5 ft back strip** | Makes that strip fall **into** the catch drain instead of over the wall |
| **③** | **Capture both downspouts** (tie into solid pipe; neighbor-side one ties in at the sump by the pump) | Removes roof water from the deck area |
| **④** | **Pipe to the street** (solid pipe from the sump, around the pool, to a pop-up at the curb) | Carries it all off-site by gravity |
| **⑤** | **Re-sod the bare strip** | Stops the sand at the source |

**Dropped from the old plan:** the fence-line **offsite interceptor (old ①)** — not needed, since no water comes from off-property. That was the biggest, most expensive piece. This revision is **smaller, cheaper, and faster.**

# FORMAT A — DIY SHOPPING LIST *(street outlet; example totals for B=30, C=150, D=2@_, E=70)*

**① Catch drain at the retaining wall**
- [ ] Channel/trench drain w/ grate (4–6", ~40" sections) — **= B** *(30 ft ≈ 9 sections)*
- [ ] Bagged concrete to bed/haunch it — **~0.6 cu ft/ft × B** *(~15–18 bags 60-lb)*
- [ ] 1 catch basin **with deep sediment sump** + atrium/beehive grate

**② Regrade the back strip**
- [ ] Screened fill/sand to set the fall into the drain — *(a few wheelbarrows to ~¼ yd³)*

**③ Downspout capture**
- [ ] Downspout-to-pipe adapters — **= D** *(2)*
- [ ] 3–4" **solid** pipe — **= sum of D distances** · elbows *(2–4)*

**④ Pipe to the street**
- [ ] 4" **solid** SDR-35/HDPE pipe — **= E** *(70 ft)*
- [ ] 1 in-line cleanout · 1 pop-up emitter (street end) · couplers, PVC primer + cement, straps

**⑤ Re-sod**
- [ ] Sod — **= C** *(150 sf)* · erosion-control blanket if the strip is steep · 6" staples

**Tools:** marking paint · mason's line + line level (or laser) · shovels/trencher (sandy soil digs easy) · plate compactor · pipe saw · drill.
**Order of work:** 811 locate → set sump + street pipe (slope ¼"/ft, 1% min) → catch drain at wall → tie in downspouts → regrade strip → sod → **flood-test.**

# FORMAT B — CONTRACTOR SCOPE OF WORK *(hand to 2–3 bidders)*

**Project:** Stop backyard runoff/sediment from entering the pool. Corner lot, sandy soil, gravity discharge to the **street (left)** — confirmed. **No offsite water; no existing drain.**

**Furnish & install:**
1. **Catch drain at the retaining wall (B ≈ *30 LF*):** remove existing decorative rock at the deck's back edge; install channel drain w/ grate set flush in concrete along the retaining wall; tie to a **catch basin with a deep sediment sump** at the low end (by the pool pump).
2. **Regrade the back strip (C ≈ *150 SF*, ~4–5 ft wide):** re-grade to positive fall **into** the catch drain — no flow over the wall, no ponding; import screened fill as needed; compact.
3. **Downspout capture (D = *2*):** connect both back-corner downspouts to solid pipe; neighbor-side ties in at the sump.
4. **Convey to street (E ≈ *70 LF*):** 4" solid SDR-35, ¼"/ft target (1% min), in-line cleanout, **pop-up emitter** at the right-of-way; comply with municipal discharge rules.
5. **Re-sod (C):** sod all disturbed/bare areas; erosion blanket on any slope.
6. **Test & closeout:** flood-test at the back strip; demonstrate flow to the street with **no water reaching the pool**; provide as-built sketch.

**Compare bids on:** licensed/insured · pulls any right-of-way permit · confirms **811** · line-item pricing per items 1–6 · stated pipe/stone/slope specs · workmanship warranty (length?) · restoration (sod, haul-off) included.

**Hybrid split option:** DIY items 3 + 5 (downspouts + sod); hire items 1 + 4 (channel drain in concrete + street line); item 2 either way.
