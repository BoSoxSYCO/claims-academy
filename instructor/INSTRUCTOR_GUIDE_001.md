# Instructor Guide — Lesson 001 FNOL

---

## Purpose

This guide supports instructors delivering FNOL training using Lesson 001 and Case 0001.

---

## Teaching Objectives

Ensure students can:

- Explain FNOL clearly
- Distinguish facts vs assumptions
- Ask proper FNOL questions
- Avoid leading questions
- Document objectively

---

## Lesson Delivery Notes

### Key Emphasis Points

- FNOL is NOT coverage determination
- FNOL is fact gathering only
- Documentation quality determines claim quality

---

## Common Student Misconceptions

Watch for:

- Assigning fault too early
- Emotional language in documentation
- Leading questions
- Skipping witness information
- Assuming coverage applies automatically

Correct immediately when observed.

---

## Classroom Exercise

Have students role-play FNOL intake:

- One student is adjuster
- One student is claimant
- Use Case 0001

Pause exercise if:

- Leading questions are used
- Fault is assumed
- Documentation becomes subjective

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Students should demonstrate:

- Neutral tone
- Structured questioning
- Accurate fact capture
- Logical follow-up questions
- Professional communication

---

## Instructor Checklist

Before moving to Lesson 002:

- [ ] Students understand FNOL purpose
- [ ] Students can rewrite biased statements
- [ ] Students can ask open-ended questions
- [ ] Students can complete Case 0001 intake correctly
