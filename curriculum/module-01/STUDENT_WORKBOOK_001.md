# Module 1 — Student Workbook

## Lesson 001: First Notice of Loss (FNOL)

---

## Section 1: Understanding FNOL

### Instructions
Answer in full sentences. Do not guess. Use Lesson 001 as your reference.

### Questions

1. What does FNOL stand for?
2. Why is FNOL important in the claims process?
3. Who can report a First Notice of Loss?
4. What is the main goal of FNOL handling?

---

## Section 2: Identifying Facts

### Instructions
Read each statement and rewrite it as a fact-based claim note.

### Example:
Bad: The driver was speeding aggressively.  
Good: The insured stated the other vehicle was speeding.

### Practice:

1. The insured caused the accident.
2. The other driver was drunk.
3. The insured is lying about the time of loss.

---

## Section 3: Open-Ended Questions

### Instructions
Rewrite each leading question into an open-ended question.

1. “You hit the car, didn’t you?”
2. “The other driver ran the red light, correct?”
3. “You were driving too fast, right?”

---

## Section 4: FNOL Scenario Practice

### Scenario

You receive a call:

> “I came back from grocery shopping and my parked car was hit.”

### Your Task

List 8 questions you would ask during FNOL.

Focus only on gathering facts.

---

## Section 5: Reflection

1. Why is it important not to assume fault during FNOL?
2. What happens if FNOL documentation is inaccurate?
3. What does “objective documentation” mean in your own words?
